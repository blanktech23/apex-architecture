 9 |
 **Legal & Compliance Exposure** |
 Medium |


- **AI liability disclaimers in ToS:** "AI outputs are decision support tools, not professional advice. All estimates, financial summaries, and recommendations must be reviewed by qualified humans before use."

- **E&O + cyber liability insurance:** Covers claims if an AI-generated estimate leads to a financial loss. Budgeted Y1: $3K, Y2: $5K, Y3: $8K.

- **Privacy Policy + DPA:** Customer data governed by data processing agreements. Tenant data isolation explicitly documented. DPAs required with Anthropic and OpenAI before processing customer financial data. Both providers offer zero-data-retention API agreements.

- **No model training on customer data:** Customer data is NEVER used for model training --- antitrust mitigation prevents algorithmic bid-rigging claims if competing contractors use the platform.

- **Lien waiver disclaimers:** AI-generated documents must be reviewed by contractor's legal counsel before use.

- **CCPA compliance:** Data access/deletion requests honored within 45 calendar days (CCPA statutory requirement), with option to extend by additional 45 days with consumer notification. Acknowledge receipt within 10 business days. Kiptra targets 30-day completion as internal SLA exceeding CCPA minimum. Data discovery spans ALL stores: Supabase (primary), Valkey (session/cache), NATS JetStream logs, Grafana Loki (structured logs), and DO Spaces backups. Identity verification required for all data access/deletion requests (email ownership confirmation + account authentication). Backup handling: daily `pg_dump` backups containing deleted tenant data are purged on natural rotation (30-day retention cycle), not individually scrubbed --- documented as a CCPA-permitted exception under the 'unreasonable effort' clause. Third-party processor deletion: Anthropic and OpenAI delete conversation data per their respective DPAs (zero-data-retention API agreements already mandated).

- **Legal counsel:** Adam (co-founder) manages legal counsel relationship for ongoing compliance.

- **Terms of Service & Privacy Policy (Wave 4 pre-launch gate):** ToS and Privacy Policy must be drafted and published before the first paying customer. Budget: $3K--$5K for legal counsel. ToS must include: AI liability disclaimers, limitation of liability, SLA terms (RPO/RTO disclosure), data ownership clauses, and acceptable use policy. Privacy Policy must include: CCPA-compliant data collection disclosure, cookie policy, and third-party processor list (Anthropic, OpenAI, SendGrid, Stripe, DigitalOcean) with data retention periods per processor.

- **Customer-facing DPA:** Template Data Processing Agreement for B2B customers (standard expectation for $5K+ annual SaaS contracts). Include in Wave 4 legal preparation alongside ToS. DPA template covers: data processing scope, sub-processor list, breach notification timeline (72 hours), data deletion obligations.

- **Contractor licensing risk:** AI-generated estimates may implicate state contractor licensing requirements in certain jurisdictions. Mitigation: all estimates are labeled "decision support --- requires licensed contractor review before submission." Legal review of top 10 target states' licensing requirements before launch. This is a Wave 4 pre-launch legal item.

 10 |
 **Competitive Response (Buildertrend / Procore ship AI)** |
 Medium |


- **Timeline:** 12--24 months before incumbents can ship equivalent multi-agent AI

- **Assessment:** Buildertrend has NO public API. Launched AI-powered Client Updates (mid-2025). Active AI roadmap under evaluation but no multi-agent orchestration capabilities announced (verified Mar 2026). Adding multi-agent AI to a monolithic legacy platform is a 2--3 year engineering effort minimum. Procore has announced "Agentic APIs" but targets enterprise GCs ($50K+/year contracts) --- serving $275/mo remodelers would cannibalize their positioning. Trunk Tools ($40M Series B, $70M total funding, Jul 2025) targets large commercial GCs with document Q&A --- different ICP entirely.

- **Speed moat:** 21-week build to production while competitors are 2--3 years from equivalent capability

- **Lock-in via workflow:** Once agents are configured for a contractor's specific processes, switching costs are high (prompt customization, integration configuration, historical data)

- **Network effect:** Ambassador network creates compounding acquisition that competitors can't replicate with paid marketing

- **Domain depth:** Agent prompts encode construction-specific knowledge (lien laws, draw schedules, AIA documents, CSI codes) that general-purpose AI tools lack

- **Pricing moat:** $275--$750/mo is below enterprise competitors' minimum deal sizes. They can't profitably serve this segment.

- **Market size buffer:** Even at 1,000 customers, Kiptra is a rounding error in the ~128K remodeling firms in the US market (NAHB data). Expansion into HVAC, landscaping, and GC verticals via the template system widens the moat further.

 17 |
 **Construction market contraction** |
 High |

 The US remodeling market is cyclical --- it contracted sharply in 2008--2009 and softened in 2023--2024 as interest rates rose. A 20% market contraction would: (1) Reduce viable target customers (fewer active contractors), (2) Cause customers to cut discretionary software spend, (3) Accelerate churn above 45% as companies downsize, (4) Reduce Adam's Slate revenue and sales bandwidth. Likelihood: Medium (construction cycles run 5--7 years). Mitigation: (a) The $5--20K setup fee front-loads revenue, reducing exposure to mid-contract churn. (b) Multi-vertical expansion (HVAC, electrical, plumbing per Section 6) diversifies beyond remodeling. (c) AI agents that reduce overhead costs become MORE valuable during a downturn, not less --- position as a cost-cutting tool when selling in a soft market. (d) Maintain 6 months of Year 1 operating reserves ($20--30K, scaling to $100K+ as monthly costs grow) from Y1 profit.
